Fraud, Economics & Integration

Why CNP Dominates
Card-not-present fraud is high because cards are used online without physical verification. Stolen card details can be used remotely.
PayTo's Different Profile
Customer authorizes agreements in their banking app with their bank's security. No stored credentials at merchant. Different risk model—but new risks emerge: agreement abuse, social engineering, merchant fraud.

Building Your Risk Register

Categories to include:

Fraud/Scams — Unauthorized collections, merchant fraud, social engineering of customers
Compliance — AUSTRAC failures, ePayments Code breaches, licensing gaps
Cyber — Data breach, API security, credential compromise
Operational — System outages, reconciliation failures, support overload
Liquidity/Safeguarding — If holding funds: safeguarding requirements, settlement timing
Reputational — Merchant failures reflecting on FairPay, public incidents

For each risk: scenario, existing controls, KRIs (key risk indicators), owner, residual rating.

Unit Economics

Cost Stack
Transaction costs (PayTo fees from sponsor) + Risk costs (fraud losses, disputes) + Operations (support, reconciliation) + Compliance (AML program, reporting) + Infrastructure (systems, monitoring) = Total cost per transaction
Pricing
Price above cost stack with margin. Consider: blended (simple) vs cost-plus (transparent). Test sensitivity to regulatory changes—if RBA shifts card costs, does your comparative value prop hold?

Reconciliation & Integration

Daily Reconciliation
Expected settlements vs actual. Handle exceptions: missing, duplicates, timing differences. ISO 20022's rich data makes matching easier—use it.
Onboarding Flow
Merchant KYB → Agreement setup → Test transactions → Go live. Design for self-service where possible.
Support Surface
What questions will merchants ask? Failed collections, disputed agreements, settlement queries. Design support tooling before you need it.
