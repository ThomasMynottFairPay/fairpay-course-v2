Rail Selection Framework

Eight criteria for comparing payment rails:

1. Availability — When can transactions happen? NPP is 24/7/365 real-time. BECS is batch (days). Cards are near real-time.
2. Data Richness — What information travels with payment? NPP uses ISO 20022 (rich, structured). BECS has 18 characters. Big difference for reconciliation.
3. Authorization Model — How does payer consent? PayTo: bank app agreements. BECS: DDR forms. Cards: card-on-file.
4. Reversibility — Can payments be reversed? Cards: chargebacks (months). BECS: indemnity claims. PayTo: final once settled.
5. Fraud Posture — Where does fraud risk sit? Cards have high CNP fraud ($816M in 2024). PayTo shifts model—customer authorizes with bank security.
6. Integration Burden — How hard to build/maintain? Cards: mature SDKs. BECS: batch files. NPP/PayTo: newer, evolving APIs.
7. Unit Economics — Cost per transaction? Cards: interchange + scheme fees + acquirer margin. BECS: very cheap. PayTo: low-moderate.
8. Access Constraints — How do you get on? All require sponsor/partner relationships.

NPP & PayTo Deep Dive

NPP Architecture
ISO 20022 messaging • Payment Access Gateways (PAGs) • 24/7/365 availability • Settlement via RBA Fast Settlement Service in central bank funds • PayID addressing
PayTo Agreement Lifecycle
Created (pending authorization) → Active (customer approved in bank app) → Suspended (paused by either party) → Cancelled (terminated)
Your system must track these states and handle transitions gracefully.
BECS Reality
Still processes huge volumes. Most existing direct debits run on BECS. The 2030 end-date was removed—transition is market-driven, not deadline-driven. FairPay strategy: Lead with PayTo, maintain BECS compatibility, don't over-invest in BECS innovation.
