Week 4

Standards & Regulatory Mapping

Navigate ISO 20022, EMV, PCI DSS, and Australian regulatory requirements.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand key standards: ISO 20022, EMV tokenisation, PCI DSS
  • Map Australian regulators and their relevance to FairPay
  • Track Treasury licensing reforms and AUSTRAC obligations
  • Build a regulatory perimeter and compliance roadmap

Technical Standards

ISO 20022
International standard for financial messaging. Rich, structured data with modelling methodology and central dictionary. NPP uses this—gives you reconciliation and reporting advantages over legacy formats.
EMV Tokenisation
Replaces card PANs with constrained tokens. If tokens are compromised, fraud value is limited. Security layer for card storage. Relevant if you handle cards as fallback.
EMV 3DS (3-D Secure)
Authentication for online card payments. Enables data exchange between merchant and issuer. Balances fraud prevention vs checkout friction.
PCI DSS v4.0
Card security standard. Future-dated requirements became mandatory 31 March 2025. As CEO: define scope, assign responsibilities, ensure audit readiness. Minimize card data handling to reduce scope.

Australian Regulatory Landscape

RBA / Payments System Board
Sets policy direction. Operates FSS. Current focus: card costs, surcharging, resilience. Conclusions expected end-March 2026.
ASIC — ePayments Code
Consumer protections for electronic payments. Currently voluntary subscription. Extended to cover NPP payments. Treasury may make mandatory via licensing reforms.
AUSTRAC — AML/CTF
If you provide designated services, you must enrol and maintain an AML program BEFORE providing services. Reforms adding new designated services with staggered start dates.
Treasury — Licensing Reforms
Payments System Modernisation Act commenced 19 Dec 2025. Tranche 1a consultation closed Nov 2025. Tranche 1b expected 2026. May capture FairPay depending on funds flow model.
