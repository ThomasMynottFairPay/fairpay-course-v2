Week 6

Partnerships, Trends & Capstone

Finalize partner strategy and complete your FairPay Payments Strategy Pack.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and evaluate key partner categories
  • Understand negotiation priorities for each partner type
  • Track external changes affecting FairPay strategy
  • Complete and present your capstone Strategy Pack

Partner Categories

NPP Access Partner (Critical)
Provides PayTo capability. Negotiate: pricing (per-transaction, minimums), SLAs (uptime, latency), API quality, support responsiveness, roadmap alignment.
BECS Sponsor
For backward compatibility. Simpler relationship. Focus: pricing, file handling, settlement timing.
KYC/KYB Provider
Merchant identity verification. Consider: coverage (ABN, director checks), speed, API integration, cost per check.
Fraud/Risk Tools
Transaction monitoring, risk scoring. Evaluate: false positive rates, integration effort, pricing model.
Compliance Advisory
AUSTRAC program setup, regulatory navigation. Especially valuable at pre-revenue stage.

Negotiation Priorities

Commercial: Pricing structure, minimum commitments, pass-through costs, volume discounts

Risk: Fraud liability allocation, dispute handling, indemnities

Data: Settlement reports, reconciliation fields, API access to transaction data

Security: PCI scope implications, incident response, CPS 234 alignment (for APRA-regulated partners)

🎓 Capstone: FairPay Payments Strategy Pack

Deliverables:

  • Slide deck (10-15 slides): Board/investor-ready
  • Executive memo (2 pages): Written narrative with assumptions

Required Contents:

  • ☐ FairPay positioning: who we serve, which problems, why now
  • ☐ Rail strategy: which rails for which use cases (with decision matrix)
  • ☐ Reference architecture: own vs partner boundaries
  • ☐ Unit economics: cost stack, pricing, sensitivities
  • ☐ Risk & compliance: regulatory perimeter, risk register highlights
  • ☐ Partner strategy: who we need, what we negotiate
  • ☐ Roadmap: next 12-24 months with external milestones

Assessment Rubric

Strategic coherence (clear choices, logical sequencing)20%
Rail strategy realism (defensible, constraints acknowledged)15%
Architecture & operability (supports reconciliation, disputes)15%
Economics & pricing (transparent assumptions, stress-tested)20%
Risk & compliance (perimeter scoped, obligations addressed)20%
Communication quality (clear narrative, explicit unknowns)10%
