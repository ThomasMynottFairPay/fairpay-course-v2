Core Payment Primitives

1. Clearing vs Settlement

Clearing is the process of exchanging payment instructions and calculating obligations. Think of it as the messaging layer—authorization requests, confirmations, instructions. This is information, not money.

Settlement is when actual money moves. In Australia, final settlement happens in "central bank money" at the RBA via Exchange Settlement Accounts (ESAs).

Clearing vs Settlement Flow
Clearing exchanges information; Settlement moves actual money through RBA accounts
Why This Matters for FairPay

When you tell a merchant "the money's coming," what are you promising? With NPP/PayTo, clearing AND settlement happen in near real-time—that's powerful. With cards, clearing is instant but settlement comes later in batches, and chargebacks can reverse it months later.

2. Finality

When is a payment truly done? When can neither party reverse it?

With PayTo, once settlement occurs through the Fast Settlement Service—it's final. The payer can't call their bank and reverse it like a card chargeback. That's a feature for collections: more certainty, less fraud risk.

Finality Comparison Across Rails
PayTo provides near-instant finality; cards remain reversible for months
💡 Key Insight

Finality is a double-edged sword. For legitimate collections, it's a competitive advantage. But it shifts responsibility to you—your merchant onboarding must be robust because there's no chargeback mechanism to catch problems.

3. Push vs Pull Payments

Push: Payer initiates and sends funds (bank transfers, Osko).

Pull: Payee initiates collection (direct debits, cards).

PayTo: Pull mechanism with payer-authorized agreements managed in banking apps.

PayTo combines pull payment efficiency with customer control via bank app agreements

Australia's Payments Ecosystem

Australian Payments Ecosystem Overview
FairPay sits in the enablement layer, connecting merchants to infrastructure via sponsor relationships
Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA)
Regulator & Infrastructure
Through its Payments System Board, sets policy for efficiency, competition, and resilience. Operates the Fast Settlement Service (FSS) for real-time NPP settlement.
FairPay relevance: The RBA shapes your environment. March 2026 update signals card cost changes coming by end-March. They also focus on resilience—think about your uptime and failover.
Australian Payments Plus (AP+)
Infrastructure Operator
Operates NPP "The Platform" (ISO 20022, 24/7, PayID, PayTo), eftpos, and BPAY.
FairPay relevance: Fundamental to your PayTo business. You connect through a participant, but understanding the platform shapes your product possibilities.
AusPayNet
Industry Body
Administers Direct Entry/BECS—legacy batch payments since the 1990s. Publishes fraud statistics.
FairPay relevance: BECS is how most direct debits work today. 2030 end-date removed—you'll need compatibility for transitioning merchants.
